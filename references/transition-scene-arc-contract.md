# Transition Scene Arc Contract

This contract prevents polished travel transitions from becoming isolated effects.

## Required Inputs

- `transition_motivation_contract_audit.json`
- `transition_pair_continuity_contract_audit.json`
- `transition_execution_readiness_contract_audit.json`
- `bridge_sequence_application_contract_audit.json`
- `transition_cadence_contract_audit.json`
- `transition_microstructure_contract_audit.json`
- `reference_scene_grammar_contract_audit.json`
- `timeline_variety_contract_audit.json`

## Pass Criteria

A reference-like travel transition must read as a short scene arc: outgoing shot, bridge or motion reason, BGM hit, title/subtitle-safe window, and a clear landing shot. Rotation, whip, push, or speed-ramp transitions are allowed only when route motion, bridge sequence, or strong pair continuity supports them.

Important route, title, timeline-gap, or ending transitions must have materialized 2-5 shot bridge beats in the final candidate. A single flashy effect cannot replace transport, street, weather, signage, window, food, hotel, skyline, or other route-texture bridge footage.

The transition arc must support the larger film grammar: movement, lived-in texture, destination payoff, and ending aftertaste must remain present. Transitions cannot be used to hide weak source choice, repeated templates, missing BGM, source-camera audio, title collisions, or stale final-candidate lineage.

## Outputs

- `transition_scene_arc_contract_audit.json`
- `transition_scene_arc_contract_audit.md`

The script is read-only. It does not write Resolve, queue renders, download assets, or modify source footage.
